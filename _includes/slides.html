CHALLENGE ROUND

How do the internally-headed relative clauses in Hindi differ from those in Bambara? The relative clause is used within the matrix clause by a demonstrative that co-references the relative clause.

Lila questions: "what's the object of the matrix verb in the Bambara (answer: the whole relative clause). What's the subject of the matrix verb in Hindi (answer: a demonstrative pronoun that is co-referential with the relative clause)." In the Barabara example, the argument of the matrix verb is the relative as a whole In the case of correlatives, it is a demonstrative that correlates/is correferential with the internally headed relative.
